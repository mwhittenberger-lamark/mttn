# Mike the Tech Ninja

## Brand Book, Website Content, Landing Page Content, and Social Strategy

## 1. Core Marketing Angle

### Positioning Statement

**Mike the Tech Ninja helps small businesses in the Triangle look, market, and operate like bigger companies without needing a big-company budget.**

The brand should not lead with “I build websites” or “I do AI.” Those are services. The stronger angle is:

**You get a senior digital strategist, web builder, AI builder, and growth marketer who can actually sit across the table from you, understand your business, and build practical tools that make your budget go further.**

### Primary Market Angle

**Big-agency brains. Local-business practicality. Small-business budget discipline.**

This gives you a clean contrast:

Large agencies are expensive, slow, and often overbuilt for small businesses.

Freelancers are affordable, but may only do one thing: web, SEO, ads, or design.

DIY tools are cheap, but they leave the owner doing the strategy and execution.

**Mike the Tech Ninja sits in the useful middle:** senior-level digital help, hands-on implementation, local access, and realistic pricing.

### Local Triangle Hook

**A local digital partner for Triangle small businesses that want modern websites, smarter marketing, and practical AI without getting sold a bloated agency package.**

This matters because AI, paid media, SEO, CRO, and automation can feel abstract or risky to small business owners. The in-person/local angle lowers the trust barrier:

> “I can meet you at your shop, office, restaurant, clinic, studio, or job site, understand how your business really works, and build the right digital plan around that.”

### Brand Promise

**I help small businesses turn limited budgets into smarter websites, better marketing, and practical AI systems that save time and create opportunities.**

### Tagline Options

**Recommended tagline:**

**Small business budget. Big business digital moves.**

Other options:

**Big-agency thinking for small-business budgets.**

**Websites, AI, and marketing that punch above your budget.**

**Smarter digital growth for Triangle small businesses.**

**Your local tech and growth partner in the Triangle.**

**Make your small business look bigger, work smarter, and grow faster.**

## 3. Brand Voice

### Voice Attributes

Helpful, plainspoken, confident, practical, local, strategic, and low-pressure.

### What the Brand Should Sound Like

Clear, experienced, and grounded.

It should avoid sounding like an agency brochure full of buzzwords.

It should sound like a capable senior digital person who knows how small businesses actually operate.

### Messaging Rules

Use plain English.

Lead with outcomes before services.

Use “small business” positively, not as a limitation.

Avoid overpromising results.

Explain AI as practical business leverage, not magic.

Make local access a trust builder.

### Words to Use Often

Practical

Local

### Words to Avoid or Minimize

Revolutionary

Synergy

Omnichannel transformation

Growth hacking

World-class

Guaranteed results

Dominate overnight

Minimal, clean, high contrast, with occasional bold blue or green accents.

# 10. First 10 Social Posts Drafted

## Post 1

Your homepage should answer three questions fast:

What do you do?

Who do you help?

What should I do next?

If a visitor has to hunt for those answers, your website is making them work too hard.

That is usually not a design problem first. It is a clarity problem.

## Post 2

Before you spend money on ads, check the page you are sending people to.

Does it match the ad?

Does it have one clear call to action?

Does it explain why someone should trust you?

Does it work well on mobile?

Paid traffic does not fix a weak landing page. It exposes it faster.

## Post 3

AI for small business does not need to be complicated.

A practical example:

Take your most common customer questions and turn them into an AI-assisted response tool for your team.

Now every reply is faster, more consistent, and still based on your actual business knowledge.

That is useful AI.

## Post 4

Small businesses do not always need bigger budgets.

Sometimes they need better sequencing.

Fix the message before the ads.

Fix the landing page before increasing spend.

Set up follow-up before buying more leads.

Track the basics before building a dashboard.

Smart order matters.

## Post 5

A good local business website should feel like the customer already walked in and met you.

Use real photos.

Show the people.

Explain the process.

Answer the obvious questions.

Make it easy to call, book, buy, or request a quote.

Trust is the conversion strategy.

## Post 6

One of the best uses of AI for small businesses:

Turn scattered knowledge into reusable systems.

FAQs. Service details. Pricing rules. Customer intake notes. Sales scripts. Past emails. SOPs.

When that information is organized, AI can help you respond, write, train, and operate faster.

## Post 7

If you are a Triangle business owner, local context matters.

A dentist in Cary, a contractor in Apex, a boutique in Raleigh, and a consultant in Durham should not all have the same digital strategy.

Your market, offer, customer behavior, and budget should shape the plan.

## Post 8

A simple lead form improvement:

Ask enough to qualify the lead, but not so much that people quit.

For many businesses, the best form asks:

Name

Contact info

What do you need?

Where are you located?

When do you need help?

That is usually enough to start.

## Post 9

The phrase “I need more traffic” might be true.

But sometimes the real issue is:

The offer is unclear.

The page does not build trust.

The call to action is weak.

The follow-up is slow.

The business is getting leads but not nurturing them.

Traffic is only one part of the system.

## Post 10

My goal with Mike the Tech Ninja is simple:

Help small businesses make smarter digital moves without forcing them into a giant agency model.

Websites, AI, SEO, paid media, email, CRO, and strategy should work together.

And they should fit the business you actually run.

# 15. Strategic Recommendation

The strongest launch path is:

1. Build the single-page website around the broader Mike the Tech Ninja brand.
2. Build the paid traffic landing page around one specific offer: Free Digital Gut Check.
3. Create a simple lead magnet: Small Business Digital Gut Check Checklist.
4. Post practical social content 3–5 times per week.
5. Run Google Search ads for high-intent terms and Meta ads for local awareness.
6. Retarget website visitors with the Digital Gut Check offer.
7. Use early calls to learn which services the Triangle market wants most.
8. Turn the first few wins into mini case studies.

The business should not be positioned as “cheap tech help.” It should be positioned as **senior digital firepower made practical, local, and affordable for small businesses.**
